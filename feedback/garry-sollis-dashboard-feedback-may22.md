# Garry Sollis — Dashboard Feedback (May 22, 2026)

## Source
Slack: #benelux-team, reply to TAM Portfolio Dashboard post

## His Key Points

### Questions/Concerns:
1. **Standby customers** — How does it handle being added to customers you're not active on (backup/standby)? Will it only focus on primary TAM customers?
2. **Time tracking = Big Brother** — Why and how is time utilization tracked? "Impact matters, not time spent." Feels like Day 2 thinking / timekeeping / performance metric.
3. **Cases ≠ Health** — Using case count as health indicator is flawed. Partner-resold customers (like Enexis) may have <5 cases/month while large direct customers (McKinsey) have >250. Doesn't mean one is healthier.
4. **Urgency vs Priority duplication** — How are "urgency score" and "priority" different? Seems redundant.
5. **Proactive should be #1** — "ProActive & Strategic should be the highest priority on any day. The rest is fire fighting."
6. **Customer priorities missing** — How are customer's own priorities reflected? From SSP? What about reactive work for health events?
7. **SSP Milestones unclear** — "AFAIK Strategic Support Planning does not have an explicit 'milestones' concept." Are they ISCs? How does it handle "not achieved"?
8. **Daily checking doesn't scale** — "We're not the customer's notification system. It also doesn't scale, even with AI."

### What He WANTS to See Instead:
- Top security & resilience risk trends (development over time)
- Outdated service versions & runtimes / extended support counts & cost over time
- A way to track if customer wants to mitigate certain risks YES/NO
- A way to dismiss suggested priority actions
- A way to surface all running escalations
- More STRATEGIC info, less tactical (not just "today & next week")

### His Summary:
> "I see a lot of tactical info (just today & next week) and not so much strategic info. As TAMs we need to be more strategic and constantly think ahead, look for the bigger picture. I don't think this does that for me."

---

## My Assessment
Garry is a senior TAM with strong opinions. His feedback is valid and constructive. Key takeaway: the dashboard is too tactical/operational and not strategic enough for experienced TAMs. He wants trend data, risk tracking, and strategic visibility — not daily task lists.

## Action Items
- Address his concerns in reply
- Consider adding a "Strategic View" section to the dashboard
- Remove or make time tracking optional
- Use CHI score for health instead of raw case counts
- Add risk trend tracking
- Make the tool flexible (dismiss actions, filter customers)
