# Live Demo Speaker Notes

## "A Morning in 5 Minutes" — Dashboard Walkthrough

**When to use:** After Slide 4, when you switch from PowerPoint to browser. **Total time:** ~5-7 minutes **Tone:** Conversational, like you're showing a colleague your setup

---

## Transition (from Slide 4 to browser)

> "Okay, enough slides. Let me show you this for real. I am going to open my actual dashboard and walk you through what my morning looks like."

*[Switch to browser — open tam-portfolio-dashboard.html]*

---

## Step 1: Open Dashboard — First Impression (30 sec)

> "So this is what I see every morning. One page. All my customers. Everything I need to know."

*[Point to KPI cards at the top]*

> "These four numbers give me instant health check:
> - I have 4 active customers plus 2 coming in
> - 7 open cases across all of them
> - 12 SSP milestones I am working on
> - And 38% of my time goes to strategic work — not just firefighting"

> "If any of these numbers look wrong — I know immediately something changed overnight."

> "And if anyone is wondering what strategic focus means exactly — hover the info icon. It clearly defines: strategic is SSP delivery, architecture reviews, proactive recommendations. Not certifications or internal stuff. Just customer-facing strategic work."

---

## Step 2: What Needs Attention (1 min)

*[Scroll to Priority Actions section]*

> "This section is my to-do list. But I did not write it — it gets generated automatically."

> "It ranks everything by urgency. Red dot means do it now. Yellow means today. Green means this week."

> "The nice thing is — when I handle something, I click this X and it goes away. It remembers what I dismissed even if I close and reopen the dashboard."

*[Hover over one to show the × button — optionally dismiss one live]*

> "So every morning this list is shorter than yesterday. That is the goal."

---

## Step 3: Engagement Gaps (30 sec)

*[Scroll to Customer Health Scorecard]*

> "This table shows me all my customers at a glance. But the column I check first is Engagement."

> "Engagement tells me: how many days since I last spoke to this customer. Green is good. If it turns yellow — I know I need to reach out."

> "I also check the CHI score — that comes from CMC — it tells me the overall health."

*[Hover over a column header info icon to show tooltip]*

> "And if you are wondering what any column means — just hover over the info icon. Everything is explained right there."

---

## Step 4: Prep for Next Call (1 min)

*[Scroll to Upcoming Cadence Calls section]*

> "Here I can see my upcoming calls. But also — look at this one for ReBound."

*[Point to ReBound completed card]*

> "This call already happened. And it shows me: what we discussed, what we agreed to do, and the key highlight from that meeting."

> "So next time I talk to ReBound, I do not need to search through emails or notes. It is all here."

> "Now here is the best part — for calls that have not happened yet, watch this."

*[Click on "BlockTech" or "Vopak" customer name]*

> "I click the customer name and it gives me a full prep panel. Open cases, recent emails and Slack messages, any health events, SSP milestones that are due, and even suggested discussion topics."

> "So in 10 seconds I know exactly what to talk about in my next call. No searching through 5 different tools."

*[Click again to collapse the prep panel]*

> "That is the assistant part of this dashboard. It does not just show data — it helps you prepare."

---

## Step 5: Mark Things Done (30 sec)

*[Scroll to Communications Requiring Action]*

> "These are emails and Slack messages that need my response. When I handle one — I click this checkmark."

*[Click ✓ on one item — watch it turn green and collapse]*

> "Green flash, then it is gone. Done. Next sync it will refresh with new items."

*[Scroll to Pending Follow-ups]*

> "Same thing here — these are things I promised to customers. When I deliver, I mark it done."

> "And where do those completed items go? They are not lost."

*[Scroll to bottom — click ▶ on "Completed Actions & Communications"]*

> "Everything I marked as done today shows up here with a timestamp. So I have a record of what I did. My manager can see it. I can see it. Full accountability."

---

## Step 6: Expand If Needed (30 sec)

*[Point to collapsed sections]*

> "You might notice some sections are collapsed by default. That is on purpose."

> "My daily view is clean — just what I need. But if I want to see SSP milestone details, or security trends, or cost data — I click here."

*[Click ▶ to expand SSP Milestone Progress]*

> "Now I can see exactly which milestones are done, in progress, or behind. Then I collapse it again."

*[Click to collapse]*

> "The idea is: show me the important stuff by default. Hide the details until I need them."

---

## Step 7: Data Sources (15 sec)

*[Scroll back to top — point to data source legend and refresh indicator]*

> "One more thing — every section tells you where the data comes from. Hover the info icon and at the bottom you see the data source."

> "And this timestamp shows you when data was last refreshed. So you always know if something is stale."

---

## Wrap Up — Back to Slides (15 sec)

> "That is my morning. 5 minutes, and I know exactly what to focus on today. No searching through 5 different tools. One page."

> "Now — let me go back to the slides for a moment."

*[Switch back to PowerPoint — go to Slide 5 "Try It Yourself"]*

---

## Tips for Delivery:

- **Scroll slowly** — give people time to see what you are pointing at
- **Do NOT explain every section** — skip ones that are collapsed
- **If someone asks a question** — answer by expanding the relevant collapsed section live
- **If demo breaks** — say "This is an MVP, things break. But you get the idea." and move on
- **End with energy** — "5 minutes, one page, full picture. That is the goal."
